2

Research Gap

01

Most studies focus on individual or operational decisions, not founder-level strategic decisions with long-term consequences

02

Decision hygiene is conceptually well-developed but rarely measured in real strategic meetings using model outputs

03

Limited understanding of how decision processes around algorithms affect psychological safety and voice in founding teams

04

Little evidence linking decision hygiene in AI-supported decisions to actual venture outcomes

3

Research Objectives

1

Understand how founders structure high-stakes strategic decisions when algorithmic forecasts are available

2

Identify and describe specific decision hygiene practices used in such decisions

3

Examine how these practices influence team climate, particularly psychological safety and voice

4

Assess whether structured decision hygiene is associated with better decision quality and venture outcomes

5

Develop practical recommendations for founders, boards, and investors on designing responsible decision processes

4

Hypotheses

H1

Teams using structured decision hygiene will make more balanced use of algorithmic forecasts than teams without such structure

H2

Decision hygiene practices will be positively associated with perceived psychological safety in decision meetings

H3

In teams using decision hygiene, more members will voice disagreement with the founder or model output

5

Hypotheses

H4

Decisions under structured hygiene will be rated as higher quality by participants in terms of fairness, thoroughness, and confidence

H5
Exploratory

Ventures reporting consistent use of decision hygiene practices will show better performance over time‚Äîfewer poor pivots, more disciplined capital use

Research Methodology

Phase I

Exploratory Qualitative Study

  • Semi-structured interviews with founders
  • Observation of decision meetings in 8‚Äì10 ventures
  • Thematic analysis to identify hygiene patterns
Phase II

Scenario-Based Experiment

  • Teams work through standardised decision cases
  • Random assignment: control vs. hygiene condition
  • Measure: forecast use, safety, voice, decision quality
Phase III

Field Survey & Archival Analysis

  • Construct Decision Hygiene Index
  • Link survey data to performance indicators
  • Regression and survival models
